Health Law Services in Nairobi, Kenya

Healthcare in Kenya is one of the most critical and rapidly evolving sectors, governed by a wide range of laws, regulations, and policies. From licensing healthcare providers to regulating pharmaceutical products and ensuring patients' rights, the legal framework surrounding health services is comprehensive and highly specialized. At WACHUKA & ADVOCATES, our health law team provides expert guidance to hospitals, clinics, pharmaceutical companies, insurers, and individuals to help them comply with regulatory requirements and safeguard their rights.

The Core of Kenyan Health Law

Our practice is anchored on the statutes and regulations that define the country's healthcare system. We provide legal support on compliance with the Health Act, 2017, which governs standards of healthcare delivery, patient rights, and professional accountability. We also advise on matters under the Medical Practitioners and Dentists Act, the Pharmacy and Poisons Act, and the Public Health Act, ensuring that healthcare providers, pharmaceutical companies, and insurers operate lawfully and ethically. Our services extend to issues of medical negligence, bioethics, data protection in health records, and public health policies, giving our clients a complete framework to manage legal risks while prioritizing patient care.

Children Law Services in Nairobi, Kenya

Children's rights and welfare are central to Kenya's legal system, with specific laws and policies designed to protect and promote the best interests of every child. Matters relating to custody, guardianship, maintenance, adoption, and child protection require both sensitivity and firm legal representation. At WACHUKA & ADVOCATES, our dedicated children law practice provides compassionate yet strategic solutions to safeguard the rights of children and to assist parents, guardians, and institutions in navigating family and child-related legal matters.

The Foundation of Children Law in Kenya

Our expertise is grounded in the Children Act, 2022, which consolidates and modernizes child protection laws in line with the Constitution of Kenya and international conventions. We provide legal guidance on parental responsibility, custody, and access, ensuring that the best interests of the child remain paramount in all proceedings. Our team also assists with adoption processes, child maintenance claims, and cases of child abuse and neglect. Additionally, we advise institutions and NGOs working in child welfare on compliance with statutory obligations, safeguarding policies, and advocacy strategies. Our services are designed to provide both legal protection and emotional reassurance in matters concerning children.

Real Estate & Property Law Services in Nairobi, Kenya

Real estate transactions in Kenya involve significant financial investment and require strict compliance with property laws and regulations. From buying or selling land and property to managing leases, mortgages, and development projects, every stage carries potential legal risks. At WACHUKA & ADVOCATES, our real estate law practice offers comprehensive legal solutions to individuals, developers, investors, and financial institutions, ensuring that property dealings are secure, compliant, and beneficial to our clients.

The Framework of Property Law in Kenya

Our real estate services are anchored in the Land Registration Act, 2012, the Land Act, 2012, and the Sectional Properties Act, 2020, which govern ownership, transfer, registration, and subdivision of land. We assist clients in conducting due diligence, drafting and reviewing sale agreements, and ensuring valid title registration. Our team also provides expert representation in matters of land disputes, succession and inheritance of property, evictions, and tenant-landlord relationships. With our guidance, clients can confidently navigate Kenya's real estate landscape, protecting their investments and securing their property rights.

Labour & Employment Law Services in Nairobi, Kenya

The relationship between employers and employees is at the heart of every business. Kenya's labour and employment laws are designed to promote fairness, protect workers' rights, and ensure that organizations operate within a framework of compliance and accountability. At WACHUKA & ADVOCATES, we provide expert legal services to both employers and employees, helping to prevent disputes, resolve conflicts, and establish strong, lawful workplace practices.

The Framework of Labour & Employment Law in Kenya

Our practice is grounded in key statutes such as the Employment Act, 2007, the Labour Relations Act, 2007, and the Occupational Safety and Health Act, 2007. We advise employers on employment contracts, staff policies, disciplinary procedures, and termination processes, ensuring compliance with statutory requirements. For employees, we provide representation in cases of wrongful dismissal, unfair labour practices, workplace harassment, and discrimination. We also assist trade unions, corporate clients, and organizations in matters of collective bargaining, labour disputes, and industrial relations. Our goal is to create workplaces where legal compliance and human dignity go hand in hand.
